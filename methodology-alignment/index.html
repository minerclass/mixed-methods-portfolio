Dissertation Methodology

Merriam-centered case design.

This studio explains why the dissertation is best framed as a qualitative-dominant convergent mixed methods case study in Merriam's interpretive tradition. The bounded case is K-12 educator and institutional sensemaking about pedagogical friction under conditions of generative AI, bounded by the phenomenon and the post-generative-AI period rather than by a single site.

Controlling Language

Merriam-centered case design governs the study. The Pedagogical Friction Framework is the interpretive lens, not the boundary of the case. Quantitative evidence, learner-perspective retrospection, and any AI artifact comparison support the qualitative case rather than redefining it.

Alignment claim

Case study defines the “what”; mixed methods defines the “how.”

The case study methodology defines and interprets the bounded educational phenomenon. The convergent mixed methods design specifies how qualitative and quantitative evidence are collected, analyzed independently, and integrated through joint displays, narrative weaving, divergence review, and meta-inferences.

Controlling Fit

Merriam

Best fit for an interpretive educational case study where the goal is descriptive and thematic understanding of a bounded problem of practice.

Comparison Lens

Stake

Useful for protecting experiential depth and lived meaning, but “collective instrumental” language can imply multiple cases. It is not the primary label.

Defense Foil

Yin

Useful for explaining what the study is not: a primarily proposition-testing, causal-explanatory, or analytic-generalization case study.

Inside the bounded case

Primary participant perspectives

  • Classroom-facing educators
  • Building-level administrators
  • District or system-level leaders
  • Institutional and public documents connected to policy, assessment, professional learning, leadership, and governance
Contextual support

Supporting evidence

  • Educator survey data, used descriptively and cautiously
  • NCES and RAND structural indicators, used as bounded national context
  • University-student retrospection, used as contextual learner-perspective support
  • Optional AI artifact comparison, outside the bounded case and appendix-level only
Defense language

One-sentence answer

“Merriam is the strongest fit because I am interpreting one bounded educational phenomenon through situated participant meaning, while the convergent mixed methods design supplies the strategy for collecting, analyzing, and integrating multiple forms of evidence within that case.”

Methodological sources

The references behind this alignment

The three-way positioning of Merriam, Stake, and Yin follows Yazan's comparative reading of their case-study traditions. Merriam supplies the controlling interpretive, bounded-case logic; Stake and Yin are retained as comparison lenses.

Merriam, S. B. (1998). Qualitative Research and Case Study Applications in Education. Jossey-Bass. See also Merriam & Tisdell (2016), Qualitative Research: A Guide to Design and Implementation.
Stake, R. E. (1995). The Art of Case Study Research. Sage.
Yin, R. K. (2018). Case Study Research and Applications (6th ed.). Sage.
Yazan, B. (2015). Three approaches to case study methods in education: Yin, Merriam, and Stake. The Qualitative Report, 20(2), 134-152.
